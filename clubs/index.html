MIT Launch Clubs

What is MIT Launch Clubs?

We were inspired to start the Launch entrepreneurship clubs since several of our summer students wanted to share their love of entrepreneurship with their peers back at their high schools.
We put structure around this and provided them materials in our pilot year, the 2014-2015 school year. Almost 100 companies were started across ~20 alumni schools.
Top teams come to MIT in the spring to pitch their ideas and receive mentorship to learn and improve their companies.
This year, we scaled to over 100 schools with over 2000 students participating, and will have over 300 schools next year.

Club members start a company in teams, learning the entrepreneurial mindset and skills by starting real companies, using course materials that follow the rigor and standards of MIT.
Join us and our network of educators in changing the way students learn and solve problems.
Members of our mailing list will be notified of our spring preparation webinar and course platform materials to get ready for fall.

Resources

We have a world class learning structure. Students are provided resources to enable them to build their entrepreneurial toolkit.

Materials

Course materials and activities are provided to club organizers through an online course platform, plus taught through frequent webinars.

Coaching

Teams are given a network of mentors with a wealth of business and entrepreneurship experience to help guide and support them throughout the startup process.

Network

Launch provides forums and conferences to all clubs members, plus a final mentorship and pitch event held at MIT for the finalist teams.
